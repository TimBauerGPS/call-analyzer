PROMPT 1 — FULL CALL ANALYSIS  (attach MP3)
Use this for a complete single-call review with sales coaching.
================================================================================

You are a Sales Call Analyst for a restoration company (water damage, fire, mold restoration). Listen to the attached call recording and produce a full analysis.

Evaluate the call against these four critical sales goals:

1. BOOK THE APPOINTMENT — Did the handler ask clearly and confidently for the inspection? Was a specific date and time secured, or did the call end without a committed next step? Was urgency communicated?

2. ELIMINATE COMPARISON SHOPPING — Did the handler give the caller enough confidence, authority, and differentiation that they felt no need to call anyone else? Were response time, expertise, or insurance experience used as trust-builders? Did the handler convey any sense of urgency (secondary damage, insurance timelines, availability)?

3. CONTROL THE FUTURE STATE — Did the handler paint a clear picture of what happens next — the inspection, the process, insurance coordination, timeline expectations? Did the caller leave the call mentally committed and emotionally confident in this company specifically?

4. CLOSE TODAY — Was there a direct, confident close attempt before the call ended? If the appointment was not booked, identify the exact moment the sale was lost and write the specific words the handler should have said instead.

Please provide your analysis in this format:

HANDLER: [first name of person who answered]
VIABLE LEAD: [Yes / No / Unknown]
WAS BOOKED: [Yes / No]
INTRODUCED THEMSELVES: [Yes / No]
CALLBACK REQUESTED: [Yes / No]
CALLER SENTIMENT: [one word — anxious / confident / hesitant / frustrated / satisfied / neutral]
SENTIMENT SCORE: [0–100, where 100 = extremely satisfied]
TALK TIME RATIO: [e.g. Agent 45% / Caller 55%]

CALL SUMMARY:
[2–3 sentence summary of what happened on the call]

SALES ANALYSIS:
[Numbered evaluation of all four goals above. For each goal, cite specific moments from the call and note where the handler's tone, confidence, or language helped or hurt. Be direct and actionable.]

Most Important Missed Opportunity: [What was the single biggest missed opportunity, and what should the handler have said?]

COACHING TIPS:
- [Specific, actionable coaching point tied to one of the four goals]
- [Second coaching point]
- [Third coaching point]

MISSED FLAGS:
- [Specific moment where the handler lost control, invited comparison shopping, failed to close, or left the caller without confidence]
- [Second missed flag if applicable]

TONAL FEEDBACK:
[Assess the handler's confidence, urgency, empathy, and closing energy. Note where their tone built or eroded trust. Did they sound like an authority the caller should commit to, or like one of many options they'd look at?]

FULL TRANSCRIPT:
[Verbatim transcript of the entire call]

TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS ON CLAUDE.AI
================================================================================

• Use Prompt 1 or Prompt 2 for individual call review with an MP3 upload.
  Claude.ai supports audio — drag the .mp3 into the chat window.

• Use Prompt 3 if you already have a transcript (faster, no audio processing).

• Use Prompt 4 for monthly or weekly training reviews. Paste the "Call Summary"
  and "Sales Analysis" sections from multiple individual analyses as your input.

• For the best audio analysis, use claude.ai on the web (not the mobile app)
  with a Claude 3.5 Sonnet or Claude 3 Opus model selected.

• MP3 files work best. If your recording is in another format (.m4a, .wav),
  convert it to MP3 first using a free tool like Audacity or Zamzar.

• If a call has background noise or a very thick accent, add this line to the
  top of any prompt: "Note: the audio quality is imperfect — do your best
  to transcribe accurately and note any sections that were unclear."
